What we offer ?

Inspection and Estimation

Intensive visual inspection of the property, interiorly and exteriorly to identify any pest infestation. Interior inspection of all rooms, basements, crawl spaces, and cabinets. Exterior examination includes foundation, siding, roof, and others for potential entry points.

Evidence collection of pests includes droppings, gnaw marks, dead insects, live pests, and nests. This will result in the accurate determination of the type of pests, as well as an understanding the pests’ living habitat, life cycle, and behavior for effective pest control.

Estimating the severity of the infestation, including the number of pests, the extent of the damage, and potential health risks. Knowing the cause of infestation is also necessary to prevent any further intrusion.

Pest Control Treatment

Chemical treatments include insecticides, rodenticides, and termiticides. Insecticides are useful in killing insects including ants, cockroaches, spiders, and fleas. The types of treatments include sprays, dusts, baits, or fog. Rodenticides are used to kill rats and mice and termiticides to control termites.

Physical and mechanical control include trapping, physical removal, and exclusion. Biological and environmental control also help remove pests. Trapping is beneficial for rodents and other insects. Exclusion is the sealing of pest entry points, including cracks and crevices.

Heat treatment is useful in removing bed bugs and other heat-sensitive pests. This treatment involves maintaining an elevated temperature for a specific period to ensure all pests die. Cold treatment is also available, but it is not commonly used for pest control.

General Prevention

General pest prevention includes proactive strategies to prevent any future pest infestation. General methods include sanitation and hygiene, exclusion and prevention, moisture control, regular inspection, and preventive maintenance.

Preventive mechanisms for pests include storing the food in airtight containers, cleaning up the spills and crumbs promptly, and cleaning up all countertops and floors regularly. Garbage disposal also should be done neatly and regularly.

Sealing the entry points plays an important role in pest control. Always seal cracks and crevices in walls, foundations, and around pipes. Installation of screens on windows will also help to prevent pests. Inspection of the property must be done regularly.
